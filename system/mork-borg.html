What is this system

An art-punk RPG about the final days of a dying world. The rules fit on a few pages, but the book is a work of art: acid colors, deranged layout, black metal atmosphere. Every session could be your last — literally, because the world ends on a schedule.

Setting

A dying world ruled by two maddened Basilisks. Prophecies foretell the end. Every morning a die is rolled — and the world inches closer to oblivion. Endless swamps, cursed villages, dark catacombs, and NO hope. But there is loot.

What it looks like at the table
Morning. Calendar of Miseries roll — a one. The Sixth Misery: 'The sun no longer sets. Shadows vanish. Without a shadow, sleep is impossible.' Your character — a Cursed Skinkling with d4 HP and a rusty sword. In the catacombs, a skeleton hits you for 5 damage. You're dead. New character generation — 3 minutes. Back into the fray.

Key mechanics

Calendar of Miseries

Every morning — a roll. On the 7th Misery the world officially dies. Campaign over. Forever.

Art as gameplay

The book is an art object. Johan Nohr created a visual language that became a genre. Dozens of hacks copy this style.

Everything is random

Character and gear are random. Classes are optional. The world spares no one.

Ecosystem

Hundreds of community supplements. Cy_Borg (cyberpunk), Pirate Borg, Vast Grimm (space) — all on the same engine.
